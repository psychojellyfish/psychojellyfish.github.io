Dave's Movie Hall of Fame: August '17

Submitted by Dave on Sat, 08/05/2017 - 12:47

Sid & Nancy (1986)

Punk Rock mythology brilliantly realized as Shakespearian Tragedy. Alex Cox’s direction captures a tangible feeling of glamour and grime. A young Gary Oldman disappears completely into his role.

It’s a punch in the gut, and that feeling never left me.

As a Punk Rocker and a Movie Fan it means more to me than I can say. To me, it deserves to be listed among the greatest films of all time.

Dr. Strangelove or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Bomb (1964)

A greater work of caricature was never committed to film. The astounding Cold War satire expertly makes fools of politicians and military with social commentary that still resonates.

Time has proved the power of its influence on our culture and social consciousness. I, for one, will quote it till nuclear winter washes over us.

I’ve never felt so amused, as well as utterly devastated, by an ending.

Pink Floyd: The Wall (1982)

I saw the movie before I heard the album. A true watershed moment of my youth that immediately expanded what I believed possible to express in art, furthering my own creativity to this day.

It’s a harsh vision, and a mesmerizing, singular experience.

Grindhouse (2007)

I had the pleasure of seeing this as intended: an over 3 hour double feature with mock trailers. Among the most thrilling, cinema fan-serving experiences of my life. Plus it was my Birthday.

It’s one massive kick. Understanding the references is part of the fun, which I was at a point to enjoy.

Death Proof is unexpectedly brilliant. If you didn’t get that then you need to read some of the great breakdowns of the film and watch it again.

Why it wasn’t successful with audiences at the time perplexes me. It certainly has reached a cult status now, influencing a whole trend of films.

Star Wars: Episode V – The Empire Strikes Back (1980)

There’s a reason this film is so revered by fans and casual viewer alike.

Building off the elements of the first film it launched an expansive saga. The characters get fully formed here, facing challenges through which they grow. It has depth, vision, personal stakes, and absolutely deserves to be called an epic.

*Bonus Short: More Human than Human – White Zombie (music video)

Zombie has his own weird style. His music is exciting, haunting, and a little greasy. This video in particular hits a real chord with me, and definitely influences my creative work.
