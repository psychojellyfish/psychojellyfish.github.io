Dave's Movie Hall of Fame: July '17

Submitted by Dave on Thu, 07/06/2017 - 14:39

It Happened One Night (1934)

It did.

I was up sick and there wasn’t anything on at that hour for a kid, so Mom put on TCM. It wasn’t The Stooges, but I gave it a shot. Soon Mom and I were laughing, totally engaged with these characters and their absurd plight. I forgot I was sick.

I often dismiss romantic comedies. It’s rare these days, but they can be great. I always smile when I think of this one.

Shaun of the Dead (2004)

This was my high school cult movie. I read about it in Fangoria Magazine, which I got at my local comic book store, and couldn’t understand why it wasn’t playing at the mall. For a while I felt like the only kid in town who knew about it, but it caught on.

Got the DVD as soon as I could and if it were a record I would’ve wore it out. It gets better with each viewing.

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles II: The Secret of the Ooze (1991)

I was all about Ninja Turtles as a kid. If I wasn’t watching the movies or cartoons I was playing with the toys or video games. They were on my birthday cakes.

While it doesn’t hold up as well as the 1st movie it’s the one with evil monsters in it, so way more fun for a kid.

The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966)

Growing up I had a name for westerns: Dad’s movies. Then as a teen I semi-reluctantly sat down for this. My perspective did a 180.

This is one of the coolest movies, period. For proof just look how it inspired coolness in movies since. Hell, even the editing is cool.

Maestro Morricone delivers hands down his most memorable score. So iconic you know it before you ever see the movie.

Alien (1979)

It’s one of the greatest artistic achievements of the 20th century, and it’s a monster movie. A perfect beast born in a perfect storm no one artist can take credit for.

A simple narrative is fleshed out by thought provoking and disturbing themes, and given life by art production that goes beyond masterful.

It changed the game on every level. Often imitated (especially in the long since creatively bankrupt franchise it spawned), but never duplicated. They don’t even come close.

BONUS SHORT: The Monsters are Due on Maple Street – The Twilight Zone (1960)

This episode is how I was introduced to Zone, and it’s got everything that makes the show great. We watched it for a grade school class and the teacher discussed its themes with us. Gee, I wonder if that had any lasting effects?
